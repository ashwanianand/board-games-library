## Overview

Love Letter is a micro card game of risk and deduction. The 21-card deck represents members of the royal court. Players hold one card at a time and draw one card on their turn, then immediately play one. Each card has a unique power — guards guess opponents′ hands, priests peek, barons compare, and the Princess wins or loses everything. The player holding the highest card at the end of a round, or the last player standing, wins a token of affection. First to collect enough tokens wins.

## Setup Notes

- Remove one card face-down from the deck (and three more face-up when playing with 2 players).
- Deal one card face-down to each player. The remaining cards form the draw pile.
- On your turn: draw one card, then play one of the two cards in your hand, applying its effect.
- A round ends when the draw pile is exhausted or all but one player are eliminated.
- The winner of each round takes one token of affection.
